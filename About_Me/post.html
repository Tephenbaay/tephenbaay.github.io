"Programs must be written for people to read, and only incidentally for machines to execute."

-Harold Abelson

Programming is an intricate art that goes beyond merely instructing machines. At its core, it is a form of communication aimed at human readers. Code should be clear, concise, and comprehensible, as it will be read and maintained by others, often long after it is written.

This human-centric approach ensures that programs are not just functional but also accessible and adaptable. While the primary purpose of code is to be executed by computers, prioritizing readability fosters collaboration, eases debugging, and accelerates future enhancements. Thus, writing code with people in mind is a fundamental principle that elevates the quality and longevity of software projects.
